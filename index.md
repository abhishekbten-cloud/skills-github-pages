# Hello
At 2:17 a.m., every satellite above Earth blinked at exactly the same time.
Nobody noticed.
They weren't looking down.
For one impossible second, every camera in orbit pointed away from the planet, toward the same patch of empty space. No signal. No star. Just black.
The astronomers called it a synchronization error.
The engineers called it a firmware anomaly.
The satellites never explained themselves.
Three months later, a probe crossed that exact point in space.
It transmitted a single image before every system onboard shut down.
The picture showed Earth.
Taken from a distance of approximately thirty billion light-years.
